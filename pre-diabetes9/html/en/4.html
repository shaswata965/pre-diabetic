Step 1: Describe the Problem.

Describe the problem in detail.

When you’re thinking about the problem, remember to:

  • Be specific.
  • Look at what led to the problem.
  • Find the action (or behavior) chain.
  • Try to see the links in the action chain. Look for:
    • Cues that make you want to eat or be inactive.
    • People who do not support you
    • Thoughts or feelings that get in your way.

Here’s an example to help you understand:

  • Mouna’s Action Chain:
    1. Mouna had an emergency meeting at work and missed breakfast
    2. When Mouna got to work, she didn’t have enough time to go and buy lunch
    3. Mouna came back home from work tired and stressed
    4. Mouna saw a box of baklava on her counter
    5. She ate the baklava and immediately regretted it
