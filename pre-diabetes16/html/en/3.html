Maintaining Weight Loss

The unfortunate truth is that most people who lose weight end up regaining it within a few years because they don’t prepare for the maintenance phase. It can be helpful for you to know that your body was not created to be in weight loss mode for long periods of time. After a while, the body’s weight loss slows down because the metabolism is reduced to support the new energy needs of a smaller and lighter body. If you’ve hit a plateau,which is a period of time when you temporarily stop losing weight, you may find that your brain is working to put back the weight on by making you have more cravings and feel less full. This is to be expected and may pose a big challenge ahead.

Studies show that the six- to 12-month mark is when most people hit a plateau and begin to regain some of their lost weight. You should anticipate this and recognize that it is not a sign of failure and that it’s natural to give up some of your losses over time. When you come to this stage, your main goals should be to shift your focus on weight loss to staying healthy.
