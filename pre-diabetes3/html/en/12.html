How to Create a Healthy Plate

On your dinner plate, imagine a line running down the middle of the plate. Then on one side, cut it again so you will have three sections on your plate.

  • Fill the largest section with non-starchy vegetables like asparagus, broccoli, cauliflower, eggplant and salad.

  • Fill the one of the small sections with grains and starchy vegetables, such as baked potatoes, corn, couscous, brown rice, quinoa, whole-wheat bread or pasta.

  • Fill the other small sections with protein, some lean options include fish, grilled chicken breast, beans and lentils.

  • Add a serving of fruit, a serving of dairy or both as your meal plan allows.

  • Choose healthy fat in small amounts.

  • Water is always your best option to stay hydrated! You can also add a low-calorie drink, or a no-calorie drink like unsweetened tea or coffee.

Droobi Tip

For cooking, stick to using healthy oils like olive oil. For salads, add healthy additions like nuts, seeds, avocado and vinaigrettes.
