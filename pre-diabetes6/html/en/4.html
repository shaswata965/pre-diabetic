Food Label

Food labels provide valuable information for healthy eating. Don’t worry, all the facts and information can be easy to understand with these tips

  • Start at the top and take a look at the serving size that’s the size of one serving. All the other numbers are based on this amount. Compare the serving size on the label with your planned portion size to know how many servings you need. Think about this example: Rawan wants to eat 5 crackers, but she reads the food label and it turns out that the serving size is 3 crackers. Rawanstick to having 3 crackers for lunch instead of 5 to prevent overeating.
  • Below the serving size is the servings per container. This will show you how many servings there are in the entire container.
  • You can also find the number of calories in a single serving. The calories are the amount of energy you get one serving.
  • Check which nutrients are high or low in a serving of food by looking at the % on the label. Pay extra attention to the total fat, cholesterol and sodium. Having too much of these can increase your risk for heart disease, cancer, diabetes and high blood pressure.
  • The list of ingredients is below or just to the side of the Nutrition Facts Label. Ingredients are listed in order of quantity, from the largest amount to the smallest. People with food allergies should always pay close attention to the ingredient list!
