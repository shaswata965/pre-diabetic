Talk Back

Here’s how to talk back to negative thoughts:


Good or bad: “Because I ate too much baklava, I will never be able to lose weight and succeed in this health program.”


Healthy living is about balance.“I am allowed to eat dessert and if I had too much I can cut back on something else. One slip-up will not ruin all the progress I have made and does not predict my future.”


Excuses: “It’s way too hot for me to exercise.”


It’s worth a try.
“I can go for a walk and if it’s too hot I can walk inside a mall.”


Should: “I shouldn’t have eaten that much during lunch. I ruined my whole day.”


It is my choice.“It was my choice to eat that much food. At my next meal time I will decide to not eat as much.”



Not as good as: “My friend lost two more pounds than I did this week.”



Everyone is different.“It is not a competition. My friend and I can lose weight at different rates and both succeed!”


Give up: “I am not strong enough to complete this program. I might as well give up.”


One step at a time.“I have learned about what is difficult for me and can use this as an opportunity to learn and grow for next time.”
