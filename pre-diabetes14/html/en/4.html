Types of Harmful Thoughts

Take a look at below to understand the different types of negative thoughts you may have had in the past:


“Because I ate too much baklava,after dinner, I will never be able to lose weight and succeed in this health program.”


Good or bad: has the view that foods are either good or bad, or they see themselves as either a success or failure.


“It’s way too hot for me to exercise.”


Excuses: blames others for problems and claims not to have any control over situations.


“I shouldn’t have eaten that much during lunch. I ruined my whole day.”


Should: expects to always make the healthy choice, which leads to being upset when failed to do so.


“My friend lost two more pounds than I did this week.”


Not as good as: compares self to others and blames self for not being like others.


“I am not strong enough to complete this program. I might as well give up.”


Give up: gets upset with self after having the other types of negative thoughts.
