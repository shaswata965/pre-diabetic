Complex Carbs vs Simple Carbs

While carbohydrates are an essential part of anyone’s meal plan, not all sources of carbohydrates are good for you. An easy way to think about it is that there are complex carbs and simple carbs.

Simple carbs are sugars in their simplest form. Some of these simple sugars occur naturally in milk, but most of them are added sugars. They are highly processed, stripped of all beneficial nutrients, and lack fiber which causes them to be digested quickly and cause a sharp rise in blood sugar levels over a short period of time. Later in the lesson you’ll learn about the major role fiber plays in helping with weight loss.

Do your best to avoid simple carbs, because they do not help in weight loss and don’t provide you with right nutrition to keep you full and energized.

You can find simple carbs in these very popular foods and drinks:

  • Anything with added sugar
  • Soda
  • Baked treats (muffins, croissants, cakes)
  • Fruit juice concentrate
  • Breakfast cereals

Complex carbs are more nutritious than simple carbs. They are minimally refined, so they still contain their vitamins and minerals. They also maintain their fiber content, which means they digest more slowly and keep you full for longer!

You can find complex carbs in

  • Whole grains (quinoa, bulgur, whole-wheat bread)
  • Beans
  • Almost all vegetables
  • Fresh fruits
