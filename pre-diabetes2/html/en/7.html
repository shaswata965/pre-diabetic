Feed Yourself Fiber

While we’re on the subject of carbohydrates, fiber is a type of carbohydrate (found in complex carbs!) that passes through the body without being digested. Let’s find out why fiber can help with weight loss and prevention of diseases like type 2 diabetes.

  • High-fiber foods, like fruits and vegetables, tend to be low in fat and calories. Because of the high fiber, they take more time to digest and cause a slow increase in blood sugar.

  • Many high-fiber foods take longer to chew and therefore allow time for your body to realize that you are full.

  • Fiber can absorb water, similar to a sponge. Because of this, the fiber in your stomach and intestines creates “bulk,” which makes you feel full longer and less likely to overeat.
