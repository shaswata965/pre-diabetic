Fat: Nourish and Repair

Fats have the highest number of calories per gram than both carbohydrates and protein.

As discussed in a previous lesson, weight loss requires you to burn more energy than you consume – so limiting your fat consumption is a great way to reduce the amount of calories you’re taking in.

Fat is needed by the body for:

  • providing energy
  • keeping your hair and skin healthy
  • helping your cells to function properly
  • protecting your body and organs
  • absorb certain nutrients
  • producing hormones

There are different types of fats. Some are healthy, others are not.

Saturated Fat in excess can increase your risk for heart disease. They are found mostly in animal products, butter and dairy products.

Unsaturated Fat can help improve your heart health. They are found in foods such as fish, nuts, avocados, sunflower oil and olive oil.

Trans fats increase your risk for heart disease. They are mostly found in processed foods.

Minimize your saturated fat intake and completely avoid trans fats as much as you can!

Droobi Tip

Bake your foods, not fry! Fried foods are one of the biggest sources of saturated fat and regular consumption can cause weight gain.
