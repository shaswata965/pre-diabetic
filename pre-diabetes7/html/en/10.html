How to Avoid Emotional Eating

  • Understand why you feel like you need to stress eat.
    For some people, stress eating can feel calming and for others it can cheer them up. Keeping a food diary to track how much you eat and how you felt afterwards can help you identify patterns of stress eating. For example, you may stop at the drive-through of a fast-food restaurant on your way home from work every time you had a stressful day. By recognizing a pattern, you can find ways to stop it. The next time you feel the need to eat fast-food because you want to feel better, think about how eating healthy and staying at a healthy weight can also make you feel better. Also, remember that the urge to eat fast food is only temporary and eating it will only provide momentary relief and not address the root cause of your stress.

  • Distract yourself.
    Having a distraction as short as five minutes can prevent you from indulging. Because the urge to eat stress eat is temporary, shifting the focus from eating to a short, enjoyable task can work wonders! Some distractions that work are:

    Go for a five-minute walk.

    Take a hot shower or bath.

    Sit outside in the sunshine.

    Listen to your favorite podcast.

    Call your best friend to chat.
