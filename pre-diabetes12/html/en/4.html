Understanding your Cholesterol Test Results

Consuming a high cholesterol diet can affect your heart by clogging up your heart’s blood vessels, which can lead to heart disease. It’s important to take a blood test to check your cholesterol levels, because if a certain type of cholesterol is too high it can impact your health.

The results of the test will show you your levels of high-density lipoproteins (HDL), low-density lipoproteins (LDL), and total cholesterol. Talk to your doctor to see if your cholesterol levels are in healthy ranges.

  1. Low-density lipoproteins are referred to as the “dangerous cholesterol”
  2. High-density-lipoproteins are known to be the “happy cholesterol”.

There are only 2 ways to increase the good type of cholesterol, HDL, that’s in your body:

  1. Exercise
  2. Taking niacin (you should only take niacin if it’s prescribed by your doctor)

Studies show that a eating a Mediterranean-type diet can help reduce your bad cholesterol because it’s typically low in red meat, saturated fat, and high in fish and vegetables. Also, sparing amounts of nuts and olive oil are known to improve your cholesterol profile.
