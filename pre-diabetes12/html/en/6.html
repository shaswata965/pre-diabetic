Blood Pressure

When you go for your regular check ups, it’s likely that you have gotten your blood pressure taken by a nurse or doctor. Do you remember what your last reading was? If you don’t, it’s okay. Let’s have you first understand what blood pressure is and how it relates to your heart.

Your heart works as a pump to deliver oxygen-filled blood to your entire body. Coming in and out of your heart are little tunnels where blood runs through. These tunnels are referred to as blood vessels. When people talk about blood pressure, they’re talking about pressure your blood puts against your blood vessels. Think about a garden hose. Water is running out of the hose at a certain pressure. When you place your thumb covering part of the hose’s opening, water is shooting out at a high pressure. The same amount of water needs to come out, but because you covered part of the hose, it needs to move harder and faster. This is the same story for plaque build up in your arteries. If there is too much cholesterol or fat covering up parts of your arteries, the pressure against your blood vessels will be higher.
