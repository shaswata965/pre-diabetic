F.I.T.T - Type

There are different types of physical activity: Aerobic, Resistance, Flexibility and Stretching, and Balance.

Aerobics is a continuous form of exercise that sustains activity. This includes walking, jogging, biking. Aerobic fitness means that your heart is doing a good job of pumping oxygen through your blood to your other muscles.

The other types of exercise include:

  • Resistance exercises

    They have been shown to improve muscular strength. An example of resistance fitness is weight lifting.

  • Flexibility (Stretching)

    Yoga is a good way to warm-up and cool down. It involves gentle movement during stretching.

  • Balance

    As you age, it is important to improve your balance to lower your risk of having a fall. Most lower body and “core” exercises can help improve your balance.

    It is best to start with exercises you can do comfortably. You can start with 5 to 10 minutes of activity, then work up to 30.
