F.I.T.T - Intensity

Intensity is a very important part of having an effective exercise session. The Talk Test is a good way to gauge intensity. Let’s take a look at a few examples to understand what different levels of intensity feel like.

If you’re casually walking at work while talking to your co-worker, you’re doing low-intensity activity. Low-intensity involves movement, but you can still talk easily while moving.

If you start to walk fast (around 100 steps per minute) to try to make a class or meet your friend on time, you’re doing moderate-intensity activity. Moderate activity will raise your heart rate, and make you breathe faster and feel warmer. Other activities that count as moderate-activity are water aerobics, biking, jumping on a trampoline, and using exercise machines at moderate pace.

If you’re on the treadmill running and cannot talk, then you’re at vigorous intensity. Doing exercise at this intensity will make you breath hard and fast. You won’t be able to speak a few words without pausing to take a breath. Other examples include jogging, running, swimming fast, skipping rope, playing an intense game of sports (basketball, football, tennis), using exercise machines at vigorous pace, boxing, karate, taekwon do, jujitsu. As you do regular activity over time, your heart will not beat as fast and so you will need to increase the intensity of your activity to get the same benefits.
