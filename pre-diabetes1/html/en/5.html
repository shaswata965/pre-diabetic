Type 2 Diabetes

Having pre-diabetes makes it more likely to develop type 2 diabetes.

Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body does not respond properly to the insulin produced by the pancreas, forcing it to make more and more insulin to keep blood sugar in a normal range. Doctor’s refer to this as insulin resistance.. If nothing is done to improve the resistance, the pancreas eventually fails to meet the demands, leading to high blood sugar, which over time can impact the body’s organs.

Type 2 diabetes can affect your:

  • Kidneys, leading to kidney failure
  • Heart and blood vessels, leading to heart attacks and strokes
  • Eyes, resulting in decreased vision or blindness
  • Nervous system, resulting in numbness or pain and abnormal sensation
  • Feet, leading to poorly healing wounds, amputation
