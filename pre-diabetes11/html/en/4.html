Triggers

Have you ever gone to the grocery store hungry and ended up buying a lot of your favorite snacks, like chips, cookies and sweets? If the answer is yes, then you were likely triggered! Seeing all the food choices while hungry triggered you to buy them. If you had gone in feeling full, it would have been less likely for you to make the same mistake.

  • Triggers can be sights, smells, sounds, or feelings that you react to in a certain way, without even thinking about it. Triggers are also referred to as cues.
  • They can also be people, places, activities, or situations.
  • Some triggers are helpful. For instance, when you touch something very hot, you let go.
  • Other triggers are harmful. These include triggers that keep you from reaching your eating and fitness goals.
