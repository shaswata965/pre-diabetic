Grains

If you are going to eat grains, pick the ones that are most nutritious. Choose whole grains for a quarter of your plate. Whole grains are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Eating whole grains regularly has been linked to reduced risk of cardiovascular diseases, type 2 diabetes, obesity and colon cancer.

Foods with grains that are processed, like fatayer, croissant, muffins, white rice, regular pasta and white bread are not as high in fiber and nutrients because the extra layers of the grain have been removed.

Grains

  • Arabic pita bread
  • Brown or wild rice
  • Bulgur
  • Chapatti
  • Freeka
  • Harees
  • Homemade, unsalted, unbuttered popcorn
  • Indian barata
  • Quinoa
  • Whole oats/oatmeal
  • Whole wheat bread
  • Whole-wheat pasta
