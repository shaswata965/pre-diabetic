Drinks to Consume and Avoid

It’s important to stay hydrated and water is simply your best choice when it comes to hydration.

Cucumbers, lettuce and watermelon have water contents greater than 90%, so you can get some of your water intake from the foods you eat!

Smoothies and milkshakes should be consumed cautiously since they may contain hidden sugars and unwanted calories unless you make them yourself using healthy ingredients (like low-fat Greek yogurt and frozen berries and veggies with no added sugars). This way you can avoid sources of calories that are disguised as being “healthy”. When it comes to hot beverages, be careful when you are ordering from a shop, because you cannot control how much sugar or cream they are putting into your tea or coffee.

What to avoid?

Avoid sugary drinks like regular soda, fruit punch, fruit drinks, energy drinks, sports drinks, sweet tea, and other sugary drinks. These can provide several hundred calories in just one serving, which can offset the calories burned during exercise that day with just a few sips!
