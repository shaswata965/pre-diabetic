Vegetables

Half of your plate should be filled with vegetables! Vegetables are full of vitamins, minerals, fiber—and with so few calories and carbohydrate, everyone can enjoy more! However, not all vegetables are created equal. There are two main types of vegetables—starchy and non-starchy.

Stick to non-starchy vegetables as much as you can. While starchy vegetables do have nutritional benefits, they can cause a spike in your blood sugar.

Non-Starchy vegetables

  • Asparagus
  • Broccoli
  • Cabbage
  • Carrots
  • Cauliflower
  • Celery
  • Cucumber
  • Eggplant
  • Kale
  • Okra
  • Salad greens (lettuce, romaine, spinach, arugula)
  • Tomato
  • Zucchini

Starchy vegetables

  • Corn
  • Green Beans
  • Green Peas
  • Parsnip
  • Plantain
  • Potato
  • Sweet Potato
  • Pumpkin
  • Squash

Droobi Tip

To get the most nutrition, eat your vegetables raw, microwaved, or steamed to help preserve the vitamins and minerals. You can also get the most nutrients by eating as many different colored vegetables!
