Fruits

Fruits are loaded with vitamins, minerals and fiber, just like vegetable sand just like vegetables, not all fruits are created equal. Dried fruits tend to have a high concentration of natural sugars, because they are dehydrated (lacking water). For that reason, they have a stronger effect on your blood sugar levels than regular fruits. For weight loss, dried fruits are best to be avoided.

You also need to be careful with fruit juices. Please note that drinking fruit juice will not provide you with the same benefits from eating the whole fruit. While some fruit juices can serve as a good source of vitamins and minerals, you miss out on the fiber you would typically get if you ate the fruit whole. Because fruit juices are lacking fiber, they can spike your blood sugar over a short period of time. It’s best to avoid fruit juices and stick with water!

Including a small piece of whole fruit or a ½ cup of fruit salad for dessert is a great complement to the rest of your meal.

Bottom line: whole fruits are the best option and should be eaten in moderation!
